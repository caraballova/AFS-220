Faqs

What Is Microblading?

A semipermanent technique for enhanching the appearance of the eyebrows, in which pigment is scratched into the skin in fine, short strokes resembling hair, using a hand tool with a blade formed of tiny needles.

It is a two-session procedure and it can be 3 months before you see final results. After the first intial appointment, you will need to return for your second session 6-8 weeks later. The second session is more important than the first because it is the adjusting and perfecting session.

How Long Does It Take?

Our appointment will be set for 2 hours.

You will be:

Numb for 30 Mins

Get Started with the first pass

Second numbing agent is applied and sits for 10 mins

Finish the brows and let the ink sit for 20 mins

You will be in and out in less than 2 hrs

Actual Microblading procedure is about 20-30 mins

How Long Does It Last?

The treatment will fade over time since its semi-permanent. However, it depends on a number of factors such as:

cell turnover rate

how quick your body absorbs it

sun exposure

skin type

chemical peels

how active the client is

how well you prep before appointment

how well you follow the post care treatment

Please note that everyone is different so every client will have different results.

Risk and Complications

I take all proper precautions for a safe session by using sterilized equipment, new blades and in a sanitized environment.

Although allergic reactions to the pigments are rare, it could be possible for certain individuals. I suggest a pigment patch test for individuals who have high skin sensitivities or are sensitive to:

Topical Makeup Products

Gold, Silver and Nickel

Hair Dyes

How Do I Prep For My Procedure?

Refrain from blood thinners such as alcohol, ibuprofen, aleve, aspirin, vitamin E and fish oil capsules for atleast 24hrs MINIMUM. No caffeine the night before or the day of. We want your muscles to be nice and relaxed.

How do I Take Care Of Them?

Strict 10 day post care treatment.

Do not wet your brows or sweat for the first 2 days. You will then wash your brows twice a day with an antibacterial soap and flat cotton pads. Afterwards, you will mositurize the brows with grape seed oil, jojoba oil or coconut oil. You will do this for 8 days (detailed instructions will be discussed during time of appoinment). You will need to stay away from direct UV exposure. No brow makeup or sunblock on the brows for 10 days. Stay away from steam such as super hot showers, saunas, swimming, etc. You will scab and be patchy for 10 days. Please note, some heal quicker than others.

What's the Healing Like?

First Week: Much darker and fuller then they will heal to be. Color will lighten as the scab starts to fall off and strokes shrink a little.

Day 7-10: Dark scabing is falling off and then the color will be super faint as if they're gone. Do not panic, this is normal.

Week 3-6 the color will start to come back but color retention is never as great as it will be after the second session, hence why the second session is more important than the first.

Does It Hurt?

Pain threshold vary person to person. Topical numbing creams and gels are applied before and during the procedure. Women on their menstrual cycle will be sensitive.

How Will I Feel and Look Immediately After The Appointment?

Everyone is different but most leave with no pain and slight tenderness. Some leave with redness and slight puffiness around brows and others leave as if a procedure was not just completed. Fair women have some redness and slight swelling but it usually does not last too long after we are done.

After My Second Session, When Will I Need Another Touchup?

Color boost is recommended 8-12 months after your second session. By doing so, we will keep your brows fresh and crisp.

Some women might need it sooner, some might not need it for another 2-3 years. Women with little to no hair will need it more frequently. Women who only needed an enhancement can go longer.

Can I Get Microblading if My Brows are Tattooed or Previously Microbladed?

Maybe!mA lot of times, strokes bleed into the tattoo and blend together. In some cases, that is great. If someone wants that natural look, they might not be able to achieve it.

Please note, I can not fix someone's brows if the shape is not fixable. For this reason, photos are required prior to booking.

In most cases, I am able to do something with tattooed brows. I focus on reshaping and color correcting. As long as the client understands that they will not have the same results as someone who doesn't have tattooed eyebrows, then we are good. They will, however, leave with a better shape (usually fuller) and a more natural color ink verses the blue, red, green, and purple healed tattooed brows.
